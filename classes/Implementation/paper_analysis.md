#  Analysis of Published Paper: Structure, Flow, and Readability

Analysing published papers that you enjoyed reading (and that are well-cited) to identify what makes them readable, engaging, and influential can be valuable for recognizing ways to develop (and improve upon) your own writing style. 
TGhe goal (and challenge) is to be able to look past their scientific contributions.


## To do (_before_ class)
Come to class having filled out [this worksheet](tex/paper_analysis.pdf) for a published paper.

Pick a paper that you think is well written and enjoyable to read.  It should have the same general format of the manuscript that you are writing (e.g., IMRaD) and have at least 50 citations (reflecting, at least to some degree, that others enjoyed reading it too).
