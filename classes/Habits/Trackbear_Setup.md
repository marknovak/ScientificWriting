# Trackbear Setup

1. Create your account on [Trackbear - https://trackbear.app/](https://trackbear.app/).
2. Create a _Project_.  
    - Click on `Projects` in the lefthand menu.
    - Click on `+ New` in the top right.
    - Give your project a `name` and select a `phase` (required -- helps organizing many projects -- but irrelevant for our course).
    - Leave `Starting balance` empty (for the purposes of our course).
3. Use the join code on the [Habits README.md](README.md) to join the "_IB 514 Scientific Writing leaderboard_".
    - Set your "big" `Individual Goal`.
        - The leaderboard is setup so that each individual establishes their own single overarching goal.  The leaderboard will show your percentage progress towards your goal.  This goal should be an encompassing (not an intermediate) goal.
        - For example, if your goal is the habit of 3 hrs. per week for the 10 weeks of the quarter, selected `time` and enter `30 h`.
        - I recommend using a time-based rather than count (words/paragraphs/etc.) goal.
    - Select the `Project(s)` that should count towards this goal.
    - (_optional_) Add tags to filter what counts.
4. Create one or more "target-specific" or "habit-forming" goals.
    - Click on `Goals`.
    - Give your goal a `name` (e.g., _"3 hrs / wk (30 hrs / quarter)"_)
    - Select `Goal Type` (I recommend using habit- rather than target-based goals).
    - Enter frequency and amount. (e.g., "_Every_ 1 _week_" and "_Time_ 3 _hours_").
        - Progress on these individual goals will count towards your overarching goal, so they can be smaller and intermediate.
    - Select the `Project(s)` that should count towards this goal.
    - (_optional_) Add tags to filter what counts.


5. **IMPORTANT** Track your progress.
    - Add Trackbear to your browser "favorites" bookmarks to make it _very quick and easy_ to enter your progress!  
    - It won't help unless you enter your progress at the end of every writing bout!
    - Use the `Enter Progress` button in the top left of the screen to track your effort.

6. You can inspect various statistics on your progress (and compare it to that of other leaderboard members) in the `Dashboard`, the `Stats`, and the `Leaderboard` views.
