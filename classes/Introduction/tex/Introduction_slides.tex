\subsection{What you'll need}

%---------------------------------------------------------

\begin{frame}
    \frametitle{What you'll need}

    \begin{quote}
    "You must be ready to start writing a scientific manuscript the first week of the term. What does “be ready” mean? 
    That’s for you and your advisor(s) to decide. 
    That said, being ready does not mean being 100\% done with your analyses or having all your final figures drafted. 
    (A key lesson of the course is that writing can and should start much earlier than that.) 
    However, you do need to be far enough along to know what your paper’s main story arc and primary message will be."
    \end{quote}

\end{frame}

\subsection{Core Principles}

\begin{frame}{Core Principles}

    \footnotesize
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Being a scientist is being a writer
        \item Productivity is designed, not discovered
        \item Structure supercedes prose
        \item Separate drafting from revising
        \item Write for your readers
        \item High-quality writing emerges from high-quality workflows
        \item Literature is managed systematically
        \item Collaboration is communication
        \item Open science and reproducibility beget efficiency
    \end{enumerate}

\end{frame}
