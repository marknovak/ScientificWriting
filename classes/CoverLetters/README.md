# Cover letters

The cover letter is pretty much the last thing you write. 
Cover letters serve several strategic and procedural functions.
Although the manuscript itself contains the scientific argument, the cover letter is your opportunity to communicate directly with the editor, framing the work and motivating editorial evaluation.
Thus, in addition to providing a concise overview of a manuscript’s central contribution and significance, it's an opportunity to convince the editor that their journal is the right and best journal to be reviewing/publishing your manuscript. 
It's also the place for you to provide any contextual information that may help the editor manage the review process, such as conflicts of interest, acknowledgement of---and differentiation with---similar work being evaluated elsewhere.
