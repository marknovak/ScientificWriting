\begin{frame}{Analysis of Peer Introductions}

  \textbf{Group format}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Work in groups of three.
    \item Spend 5--10 minutes reading each peer's introduction draft.
    \item For each of your two peers, spend 15 minutes writing feedback.
  \end{itemize}

    After everyone is finished (35--40 minutes), take up to 10 minutes to explain your answers to each peer (about 30 minutes total).

    \begin{block}{Focus of this activity}
    We are not evaluating scientific correctness.
    We are evaluating clarity, structure, and audience engagement.
  \end{block}

\end{frame}

% ============================================================
\begin{frame}{Feedback Questions}


  \textbf{For each peer's introduction, answer:}
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item What did the author do well?
    \item Was the topic introduced compellingly and at the right level?
      Suggest one revision to improve engagement and/or audience reach.
    \item Was the manuscript's specific question clear?
      Paraphrase it in 1--3 sentences.
    \item Was the logical flow clear? Summarize logic in 4--8 words
      (e.g., x $\rightarrow$ y $\rightarrow$ z).
      Identify one place that could be clarified.
    \item If the Introduction ended with a take-home message,
      what was it? Paraphrase in 1--3 sentences and assess whether
      it matched expectations created by the rest of the Introduction.
  \end{enumerate}

\end{frame}

% ============================================================
\begin{frame}{Debrief and Reflection}

  \begin{itemize}
    \item What were common suggestions?
    \item What did you learn about your own writing?
    \item What characterized helpful feedback?
  \end{itemize}

\end{frame}
